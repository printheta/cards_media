Midnights (3am Edition) - Bonus Tracks

3 hours after the release of Midnights, with its 13 original tracks, it is followed by the release of the 3am Edition adding 7 bonus tracks in the album.

  • The Great War
  • Bigger Than The Whole Sky
  • Paris
  • High Infidelity
  • Glitch
  • Would've, Could've, Should've
  • Dear Reader
“The Great War” is a bonus track off Taylor Swift's Midnights album. It details a devastating conflict within a romantic relationship. Despite the conflict, “The Great War” delivers a hopeful message, describing a couple emerging from the tension together and promising to love one another “always” since they have already survived a a difficult period together.
“Bigger Than The Whole Sky” is the fifteenth track off Midnights (3am Edition) and the second bonus track on the album, which was surprise released at 3am, three hours after the original Midnights release. “Bigger Than The Whole Sky” is a song about losing someone important to you despite only spending a short time with them and wondering what it would have been like if things had turned out differently. Swift leaves the sort of loss open to interpretation.
Paris is the sixteenth track on Taylor Swift's tenth album, Midnights. It was released as apart of the surprise drop of Midnights 3AM edition and is the third Taylor swift song to have a city in the title - the others being “London Boy” and “Welcome To New York”.
“High Infidelity” continues a pattern of Taylor exploring the nuances of cheating and infidelity, which started in 2020's folklore and includes songs such as “illicit affairs”, “august”, “betty”, “no body no crime”, and “ivy”. In “High Infidelity”, Taylor delves in-depth into the games played within an unstable relationship and the intense pain of infidelity, caused both to the cheating partner and the partner being cheated on.
“Glitch” reflects on a relationship that was never meant to be. It started off through friendship, casually evolved into friendship with benefits, but somehow turned into a true romance, so unlikely that it must have been a glitch.
In “Would've, Could've, Should've”, Taylor uses morbid and religious overtones in order to express regret for a youthful relationship that left her deeply scarred. This draws on themes such as those in previous songs “A Perfectly Good Heart” and “Dear John”, where the singer's naivete was similarly trampled on.
“Dear Reader” is the final track of the 3AM edition of Taylor Swift's 2022 album Midnights. In it, Taylor attempts to give the listener (or reader) life advice. She advises the listener to, among other things, be wary, reinvent themselves, bend when they can but not bend over for others who ask of them, and keep secrets. These are all meant not to be trusted, as the narrator themself warns the listener not to. In the song “Dear Reader”, the narrator has come into a very isolated, desperate life, which provides a fitting finale to an album touching on self-doubt, mistrust, and pain throughout.
